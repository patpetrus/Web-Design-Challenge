Comparisons

Visualizations

When compared against the factors of wind speed, cloudiness, and maximum temperature, one notes that latitude offers at best selective influence. Its effect is most prominently noticed when compared to max temperature; temperatures trend up in cities close to the equator, whereas they drop as they near the poles, with the northern hemisphere trending colder than the southern.
Humidity and cloudiness both offer little to no correlation, as the data is more or less equally distributed across the graph. A larger, though still not very significant, correlation can be observed when comparing latitude to wind speed. Cities falling above forty degrees latitude have a slightly higher than average wind speed than theh rest of the world, suggesting stronger weather patterns in the north of the planet than the south.
